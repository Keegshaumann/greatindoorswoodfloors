Stain colors for every taste

We stock stain swatches from Bona, Duraseal, and Minwax, and mix custom colors when nothing standard fits. We'll help you find the tone that suits your home and your taste.

Call (317) 403-1526 See finished floors

Sampled on your floor, not on a chip

Printed charts get you close, but the final decision happens on site. We brush candidate colors right on your freshly sanded wood so you see each one in your own light.

Pick a favorite, and the rejects sand off easily. If no standard color pleases you, we mix custom stains from clear or white bases for a wide range of hues.

We're part of the Bona Certified Craftsman program and use Bona topcoat finishes to seal and protect the color you choose. More on stains and finishes on our FAQ page.

How choosing a color works

  • Sand to raw wood so every sample shows its true color
  • Test the front-runners directly on your floor
  • Sand off the rejects, they come off easily
  • Stain and seal with durable Bona topcoat finishes

Three brands, hundreds of colors

These basic color charts come from each maker's website. Scroll inside the taller charts to see the full range, then let's narrow it down together.

Getting the color just right

The wood itself has a say. Its natural color shines through the stain, mixes with it, and creates a new color all its own. We plan for that.

When a color is close but not quite deep enough, a technique called water-popping lets us darken a stain by a few degrees while keeping its balance of color.

Color work runs through everything we do, from full refinishing to whitewashed floors and oil-finished floors, where craft oils bring color and protection in one coat.

Spotted a color you like?

Call for a free consultation. We'll help you choose a standard color or mix something custom, then sample the front-runners right on your wood.
