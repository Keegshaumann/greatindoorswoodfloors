Wood floor refinishing, honestly answered

The same good questions come up before nearly every refinishing job. Here are the plain answers, from thousands of Indianapolis floors since 1994.

The questions we hear most

Tap a question to open the answer. If yours isn't covered, call or email and we'll give you a straight one.

How long does a wood floor refinishing project take?

A typical residential sand and refinish takes 2–3 days for a normal sized house.

Many clients schedule the work while they're out of town on vacation. It's the most convenient way to handle a project.

Can't leave? We work around each other and still finish on time, on budget, and to your satisfaction.

Do I need to move my furniture before refinishing?

Yes. All furniture needs to be out of the rooms being refinished before we start.

We also need easy access to bring in our equipment.

How do I protect my new floors from furniture legs?

Put floor protection on every piece of furniture going back on the new floor. Nail-in or tap-in protectors with wool pads work best.

We're happy to supply and install them for you at an additional cost.

Should I choose a water-based or oil-based finish?

Water-based, for most jobs. It dries faster and is more durable than oil-based finish.

You can walk on it lightly in 1–2 hours. Furniture, with pads and no sliding, can come back in 12–24 hours. The surface is roughly 85% cured in 24 hours.

Water-based finishes also dry consistently no matter the temperature or humidity.

Oil-based finishes dry slower, are drastically affected by humidity, and aren't as durable. We use them only to match an existing oil-based floor, or when a client wants the antique look and ambering a thick oil coat creates.

What are craft oil finishes?

Another fine option. Craft oils penetrate deep into the wood grain, offering natural protection and a matte finish.

Craft oils and hard wax oils are environmentally green. There's more on our oil-finished floors page.

How do stain colors work?

We carry stain swatches from Duraseal and Minwax, mix custom colors, and specialize in matching existing floors.

The wood's own color shines through the stain and mixes with it, creating a new color all its own. So we sample colors on-site, right on your raw sanded wood.

Samples sand off easily once you've picked a winner. And a technique called water popping lets us darken a color a few degrees while keeping its balance.

Browse our stain colors page for ideas.

How should I clean my wood floors?

Cleaning kits and instructions are available on request, just ask.

For years of ground-in grime, see our deep cleaning service.
